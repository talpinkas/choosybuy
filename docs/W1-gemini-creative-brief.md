CREATIVE BRIEF — SHORT ANIMATED BRAND FILM FOR "CHOOSY"

Please act as an expert animation studio. Read this whole brief, plan the film, and
produce it using your video tools. This is a creative requirement document, not a
list of technical settings — interpret it with craft and taste.

== THE PRODUCT ==
Choosy is an online way to choose kids' clothing without the overwhelm. Instead of
scrolling through endless options, you simply compare TWO items at a time and pick
the one you like better, again and again, until a single winner remains. It turns a
long, tiring, frustrating shopping task into something fast, easy, smart and fun.

== GOAL OF THE FILM ==
In about 30–35 seconds, make a parent instantly feel the contrast: online clothes
shopping today = drowning in options and wasting time; Choosy = find what you want
in a moment, calmly and joyfully. The viewer should finish thinking "I want that."

== AUDIENCE & USE ==
Busy parents. The film will run on vertical mobile feeds (WhatsApp status, Instagram
Reels/Stories) and on the website. It must be understandable with the sound off and
in any language — so it is WORDLESS: no dialogue, no narration, and no on-screen text
at all, except the two brand end cards described at the end.

== FORMAT ==
- Vertical 9:16, full-frame mobile.
- About 30–35 seconds.
- Smooth, gentle, characterful 2D animation.

== VISUAL STYLE (very important, keep consistent throughout) ==
- A minimalist, hand-drawn 2D cartoon in a warm children's-book style: clean thick
  black outlines, simple flat shapes, rounded friendly non-realistic characters,
  lots of white space, flat lighting, no gradients, no photorealism.
- A signature color device: the world is drawn in black, white and grey. Color is
  used ONLY with meaning (see each scene). This selective color is the heart of the
  film — Choosy is what brings color and life back into a grey, overloaded world.
- Characters, kept identical the whole film: a young, relatable mother in her early
  30s (warm friendly face, dark shoulder-length hair, casual sweater); her small
  toddler son; and briefly an older daughter.

== EMOTIONAL ARC ==
Busy normal life → frustration and wasted time → a spark of hope → calm, confident,
delightful choosing → pure joy. The music (soft, warm, hopeful instrumental, no
voices) follows this curve: light tension that builds, then lifts and resolves warmly.

== THE FILM, SCENE BY SCENE (with meaning and message) ==

SCENE 1 — THE TRIGGER (~4s)
What we see: A cozy living room. The mother sits at a desk, busy and relaxed, working
on her laptop. Her little son walks up to her and holds up a faded, slightly worn-out
green t-shirt with a small tear — he has outgrown / ruined it and needs a new one. The
torn green shirt is the ONLY thing in color; everything else is greyscale.
What it represents: the small, everyday, real-life parenting need that starts it all.
Message: life is already full and busy; another task just landed.

SCENE 2 — THE ENDLESS SEARCH & WASTED TIME (~7s)
What we see: The mother turns to her laptop and starts shopping online. The screen
fills with an endless grid of clothing — and it keeps scrolling, faster and faster,
never ending. To show how much time slips away, the round wall clock's hands spin
around quickly, and through the window behind her the sky cycles from bright day to
night to day again, the sun setting and rising over and over.
What it represents: online shopping = an ocean of options and time quietly draining.
Message: the search is exhausting and it steals your time. (Everything stays grey
except the green shirt.)

SCENE 3 — OVERWHELM PEAKS (~5s)
What we see: Options pile up and crowd around her. At the same time life piles on too
— a phone rings and buzzes on the desk, the older daughter walks in and tugs at her
sleeve, the toddler still waits. The mother slumps, worn out, head in her hand.
What it represents: the breaking point of decision overload colliding with a busy life.
Message: it's simply too much.

SCENE 4 — CHOOSY ARRIVES, THE TURN (~3s)
What we see: A pause. The laptop screen lights up with a warm, colorful glow, and the
Choosy logo appears there in full color — the first burst of color into the grey world.
A small spark of hope lifts the mother's face (shown only through her expression, not
a colored mark on her). She sits up.
What it represents: a better way appears.
Message: relief — there is another option.

SCENE 5 — THE CHOOSY METHOD, THE MAGIC (~8s)
What we see: We move in to see the screen clearly (from a natural angle where she is
in front of it). Choosy shows the simple game: two shirts side by side. She looks and
taps the one she likes. A fresh pair of two shirts appears — she taps again. Another
pair — she taps. A few calm, quick, confident rounds, every option a shirt, until one
favorite shirt wins, marked with a check. With each choice, warm color spreads out
from the screen into the room.
What it represents: the core idea — compare two, not many; easy, fast, sure decisions.
Message: you don't search, you simply choose between two, until you find the one. This
is the product's promise — show it clearly, as a real little sequence, not a single tap.

SCENE 6 — INSTANT RESULT & DELIVERY (~4s)
What we see: The moment she chooses, a doorbell rings. At the front door waits a
delivery package. She opens it and lifts out a brand-new t-shirt — this one a vivid,
bright, glowing green.
What it represents: a fast, satisfying outcome with no friction.
Message: found it, done, and it arrives — almost instantly.

SCENE 7 — JOY & THE COLOR BLOOM (~4s)
What we see: She slips the bright green shirt onto her son, who bursts from crying into
laughter. Starting from that green shirt, a warm wave of full color blooms outward and
fills the entire room — the once-grey world comes alive with gentle, happy color.
What it represents: happiness and relief; a draining chore turned into a joyful win.
Message: Choosy gives you back your time, your calm, and the joy.

CARD 2 (about 3s), cream background, centered:
   [the Choosy logo — a rounded burnt-orange square containing two small "o" shapes,
   one filled and one a ring, in cream] then the wordmark "CHOOSY" in burnt orange,
   then the line: Stop searching, start finding.
   then the website: choosybuy.com
(If you cannot reproduce the exact logo, leave a clean centered space for it and we
will place the real logo afterward — do not invent a different logo.)

== BRAND COLORS ==
Burnt orange #ce551f (primary, the Choosy color/energy), warm cream #fbf5ea
(backgrounds), dark warm brown #33271e (text), and a fresh bright green for the
"found" shirt. Keep everything else greyscale until color blooms in.

== WHAT TO AVOID ==
No spoken words, no narration, no on-screen text except the two end cards. No literal
or surreal mistakes (e.g. clothes coming out of a ceiling fan) — keep actions natural
and readable. No photorealism. No invented brand logos. Keep the characters and the
art style perfectly consistent from the first frame to the last.
